Bio Assessment

The bio is functional and informative — it clearly lists service times (Sunday 11h & 19h), Bible Study (Sunday 10h), the physical address with a helpful proximity note to the Pinheiros metro station, and parking availability. However, it lacks a URL link to a website, WhatsApp, or Linktree where visitors could take a next step (e.g., contact, join a small group, donate). The Facebook link is present but may not be the most effective CTA for a younger audience.

Profile Photo

The logo is clean and legible — the green shield with "Primeira Igreja Batista de Pinheiros" is recognizable and professional.

Content Strategy

The account relies almost exclusively on Reels (short videos). In the 7 most recent posts reviewed, 6 were Reels and 1 was a Carousel (multi-photo post for the church anniversary). There were no static single-image posts in recent content, which aligns well with Instagram's current algorithm preference for video.

Content Pillars Observed

Worship Moments

Reels capturing Sunday services, with praise music, congregation shots, and scripture captions. This is the dominant content type.

Announcements / Events

Designed graphic Reels promoting specific events (e.g., the Easter Musical "A Cruz: Espetáculo da Vida").

Ministry Spotlights

Youth group (via the @habitejuventude collaboration), Women's Day celebration.

Milestone Commemorations

The 76th church anniversary carousel.

Positives

The content pillars are coherent and well-aligned with the church's identity. Captions consistently include a relevant Bible verse, which reinforces the spiritual tone and gives followers devotional value beyond just the visual. The writing style is warm and accessible.

Gaps

There is very little content variety beyond worship services. Missing content types that could diversify and grow the profile include: pastoral teaching clips or devotionals, behind-the-scenes of ministries, testimonials, community outreach content, Q&A or interactive formats, and seasonal devotional series.

Issues Identificados

The hashtags are all brand or category-level — there are no location-based hashtags (e.g., #igrejasaopaulo #Pinheiros #SP) or trend-adjacent tags (e.g., #fé #devocional #palavradeDeus) that could attract new audiences outside existing followers.

Ideal (3–5x/semana)

07

Recommendations

Bio & Profile

Add a link — ideally a Linktree or a direct WhatsApp link — so visitors can take immediate action (visit the church, contact leadership, join a group). Consider adding a clear call-to-action line in the bio such as "Venha nos conhecer 👇."

Content Mix

Introduce at least 2–3 new content types: short pastoral reflection videos (30–60s clips of the pastor with a devotional thought), congregation testimonials (even 15-second clips), and ministry introductions (showing EBD, worship team, etc.). This will attract new visitors who may not yet feel ready to attend in person.

Hashtags

Expand to 12–15 hashtags per post. Include local hashtags like #igrejasaopaulo #Pinheiros #igrejaemSP and thematic/devotional ones like #palavradeDeus #devocional #fécristã. This is one of the easiest and highest-impact changes to implement immediately.

Call-to-Action in Captions

End every caption with a question or CTA to drive comments — for example: "Como Deus tem te lembrado da Sua presença essa semana? Conta pra gente nos comentários 💬". This alone could meaningfully increase comment counts and boost algorithmic distribution.

Stories

Stories were not audited but are critical — they drive daily visibility and keep the community engaged between posts. The account should post daily Stories covering: announcements, behind-the-scenes moments, polls, scripture of the day, and reminders about service times.

Posting Frequency

Aim for 3–4 posts per week. A simple editorial calendar could be: Monday devotional Reel, Wednesday ministry spotlight or announcement, Saturday worship preview, Sunday worship recap.

Explore Cross-Platform

The Facebook page is linked but there's no website URL. A simple landing page (even a Neon or Linktree) aggregating service times, event registration, and a WhatsApp contact would reduce friction for first-time visitors discovering the church through Instagram.
